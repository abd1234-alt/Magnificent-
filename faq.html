Common Questions

Answers to your
most asked questions

Can't find what you're looking for? Call us on +234 703 316 3621 or send us a message.

Do I need a referral to register at Magnificent God Maternity Center?
+

No referral is needed. You can walk in or book an appointment directly via our website, phone, or WhatsApp. We welcome all patients, whether referred or self-registering.

What are your opening hours?
+

Our outpatient clinic is open Monday to Saturday, 7:00am to 8:00pm. Our emergency maternity unit, delivery suite, and ward are open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week — including public holidays.

Where exactly are you located?
+

We are located at No. 4, Waheed Abiola, Sule Close, Shapati, Ibeju Lekki, Lagos State, Nigeria. We are easily accessible via the Lekki-Epe Expressway. Patient parking is available on-site. See the map on our Contact page.

Can my husband or partner be present during delivery?
+

Yes. We strongly support the presence of a birth partner during labour and delivery. Partners are welcome in the delivery suite. For caesarean sections, a birth partner may be present at the discretion of the medical team.

When should I register for antenatal care?
+

We recommend registering as soon as you confirm your pregnancy — ideally within the first 8 to 12 weeks. Early registration allows us to screen for any risks and create the best care plan for you and your baby from the very beginning.

What does the antenatal registration package include?
+

Our antenatal package includes all scheduled clinic visits with our midwife and obstetrician, routine blood tests, blood pressure monitoring, urinalysis, foetal heartrate checks, antenatal education classes, nutritional counselling, and birth planning sessions. Ultrasound scans are available at a separate fee unless you choose our all-inclusive package.

How many antenatal visits will I need?
+

For a low-risk pregnancy, the WHO recommends a minimum of 8 antenatal contacts. At MGMC, we typically schedule visits at 8–12 weeks, 16, 20, 24, 28, 32, 34, 36, and then weekly from 38 weeks until delivery. High-risk pregnancies may require more frequent monitoring.

Is the facility equipped for emergency caesarean sections?
+

Yes. We have a fully operational theatre with experienced surgeons, anaesthesiologists, and theatre nurses available 24 hours a day. Emergency C-sections can be performed within minutes of the decision being made, in line with international obstetric emergency standards.

What should I bring when I come in for delivery?
+

Please bring your antenatal card and health records, a valid ID, your NHIS card or HMO documents if applicable, baby items (vest, baby grow, diapers, blanket), personal toiletries, comfortable nightwear, and any medications currently prescribed. We will provide a full delivery pack list when you register.

How long will I stay after delivery?
+

Most mothers who deliver normally are discharged within 24–48 hours after careful postnatal assessment of mother and baby. After a caesarean section, the typical stay is 3–5 days. Your doctor will advise based on your individual recovery.

Do you accept NHIS (National Health Insurance)?
+

Yes, we are a registered NHIS facility. We also accept various HMO plans. Please bring your NHIS card or HMO details at registration. For patients without insurance, we offer transparent pricing and flexible payment arrangements — please speak to our billing desk.

What payment methods do you accept?
+

We accept cash, POS card payments (Visa, Mastercard), bank transfers, and mobile payments (Opay, PalmPay). We also accept payments through approved HMO and NHIS channels. A payment receipt is issued for all transactions.

Do you offer payment plans for delivery packages?
+

Yes. We understand that maternity costs can be significant, and we offer structured payment plans for mothers who register early. Payment can be spread across the antenatal period so that the balance is settled before the expected delivery date. Please ask our billing team for details during registration.

What should I do if I have an obstetric emergency at night?
+

Call our emergency line immediately: +234 703 316 3621. Our emergency team is on duty 24/7 and will guide you over the phone while you make your way to us. Do not delay — come straight to our Emergency Maternity Unit at the ground floor entrance on Hospital Road.

What warning signs during pregnancy require urgent care?
+

Seek emergency care immediately if you experience: heavy vaginal bleeding, sudden severe headache or visual disturbances, significant swelling of face, hands or feet, baby not moving for several hours, severe abdominal pain, fever above 38°C, leaking of fluid from the vagina before 37 weeks, or any other sudden change that worries you. When in doubt, call us.
